Zon, zee en strand

Tuesday, September 21, 2004

Police

Today we rented a car to go south to Salvador and Chapadas de Diamantines. Our friend Martijn told us about highway police controls and that they like to ´earn money´. So at the first police stop we were stopped by a very impressive looking officer with a serious shotgun. He wanted to see my driver license and the car papers. I gave them and he asked for a carte de translitacao (or something like that) - it turned out to be the translation of my driver license - I didn´t understand what he wanted, nor did I have it. I knew that my dutch driverlicense was vald for a month without the translation or a brasilian driver license.
The officer, who was actually not really tall invited me to sit on a table and three other equally impressive officers talked to my in portuguese. Even though I could understand a bit of their conversation I veigned utter ignorance on their purpose. A friendly brasilian who spoke english tried to translate but the moment he saw the police were trying to get money from me he quickly left.
The amount of money went from 3 euro for the translation to a fine of 100 euro, which I myself had to recalculate into brasilian reals for them. I was not allowed to drive and had to call someone to pick me up etc. Not entirely sure I called Martijn to check if I really did need the translation or not. He said no you don´t, smile a lot and be friendly.
So I went back to the officers politely tolled them that I was in my right and weighted for the things to come. They walked a bit in circles around me, prefering me to sit, since otherwise they had to look up :).
In the end one guy took me to a seperate room, acted like he was doing me a favour, did the universal money and drinking symbol, I looked blankely at him. Apparently they decided I wasn´t worth their time (about an hour) and they let me go.
I love this country, the people are /so/ friendly ;)
